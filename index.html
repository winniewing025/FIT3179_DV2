How Malaysia's Agricultural Heartland Fuels the Nation's Economy
An analysis of the relationship between agricultural production and economic growth across Malaysian states

Introduction

Agriculture is a key sector in Malaysia, but its contribution varies significantly across states. This analysis explores which states are agricultural powerhouses, what they grow, and how this relates to economic performance.

Key Findings:

  • High agricultural production doesn't always correlate with strong GDP growth
  • Johor leads in total production but shows modest economic growth
  • Pahang achieves the highest GDP growth despite moderate production
  • Eastern states face economic challenges despite agricultural activities
